Research interests

Research in our group is focused on developing and applying multiscale analytical and computational methods to study soft matter - i.e. substances held together by relatively weak non-covalent interactions, such as liquids, liquid crystals, polymers, gels, colloids, surfactants, membranes, biomaterials (e.g. DNAs, proteins and lipids) and their composites - from a molecular and nanoscale perspective. Such studies are relevant for our fundamental understanding of matter and the design of novel materials with applications in areas including health and renewable energy. To inspire this work and to stimulate research in real systems we often work closely with experimentalists.

Nanobubbles

Nanobubbles are nanoscale gas-containing cavities on the liquid-solid surfaces (surface nanobubbles) or in the bulk aqueous solutions (bulk nanobubbles). These novel nanoscale bubbles exhibit a lot of unusual properties (e.g. extreme stability, abnormal contact angle) which challenge our understanding of the bubble behaviors at nanoscale regime.

Phoretic Transport

Phoretic transport represents the motion of colloids/molecules in fluids and the fluid flow near solid surfaces induced by a electric field, a concentration gradient or a temperature gradient (i.e. electro-/diffusio-/thermo-phoresis and electro-/diffusio-/thermo-osmosis). The phoretic transport processes offer important advantages in fields as diverse as micro-/nano-fluidics, colloid science and separations because of the controllable processes may be achieved through surface structure and chemistry.

Molecule/Nanomaterial Self-Assembly

Self-assembly describes the processes by which individual units come together to form organized structures, mediated by individual particle interactions or globally applied external forces such as electric/magnetic fields or fluid flow. Understanding self-assembly allows us to fabricate materials through a bottom–up approach, which provide a precise control on the structures to achieve desired properties.

Membrane Materials

Membrane materials act as the core role in gas and liquid separations. Acquiring simultaneous enhancement of permeability, selectivity and stability is a critical scientific issue in the field of membrane materials. Solving this issue involves a lot of more fundamental problems such as the formation of materials, the fluid flow through micro-/nano-pores.
